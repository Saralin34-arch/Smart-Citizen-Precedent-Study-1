Three Layers of Analysis

1. Sensing Layer

What it is: The foundation of the Smart Citizen ecosystem. This layer consists of open-source hardware devices (Smart Citizen Kits) equipped with sensors for air quality, noise, temperature, humidity, and more.
How it works: These kits are deployed in homes, schools, and public spaces, enabling citizens to collect high-resolution, real-time environmental data.
Why it matters: It democratizes data collection, making environmental monitoring accessible to everyone and fostering a sense of ownership and participation.

2. Data Platform Layer

What it is: The Smart Citizen online platform and API.
How it works: Data from all kits is sent to a central platform, where it is aggregated, visualized, and made openly accessible. Users can explore interactive maps, download datasets, and use APIs for custom analysis.
Why it matters: This layer ensures transparency, open access, and interoperability, allowing anyone to use the data for research, education, or advocacy.

3. Community & Insights Layer

What it is: The collaborative and participatory aspect of Smart Citizen.
How it works: Communities, researchers, and policymakers use the platform to launch projects, share findings, and co-create solutions. The platform supports workshops, hackathons, and citizen science campaigns.
Why it matters: This layer transforms raw data into actionable knowledge, empowering communities to address local challenges and influence policy.
